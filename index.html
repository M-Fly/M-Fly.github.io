M-Fly Student Aero Design Team

Who We Are

The M-Fly Student Design team is a multidisciplinary organization whose mission is to design, build and compete aircraft. Our team consists of members from several major and classes from the College of Engineering. As a team, our vision is to promote aircraft design, education, and we focus on helping our team members and students grow as citizens.

What We Do

Every year, we build two planes to compete in the “Society of American Engineers (SAE) Aero Design Competition”. Our SAE planes focus on high-lift and payload delivery applications. These planes incorporate various technologies, including composite materials and autonomous payload gliders. We also build a third plane to compete in the “Association For Unmanned Vehicle Systems International Student Unmanned Aerial Systems Competition” (AUVSI-SUAS), with a goal of building an unmanned aerial system that is capable of autonomous flight and navigation, remote sensing, and the execution of specific tasks.

How to Get Involved

We meet regularly at the Wilson Student Team Project Center at the University of Michigan. If you’re interested in joining our team, head to our “Join Us” page. Also, feel free to stop by our General Team Meeting, which are held every Wednesday from 6:30 PM to 7:00 PM in 185 EWRE.
